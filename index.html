Detecting Gerrymandering with Markov Chain Monte Carlo

Using statistical simulation to test whether electoral maps are partisan outliers — and interrogating why shape alone cannot tell us.

Lucas · Mentored by Prof. Bo Zhao, Humanistic GIS Lab, University of Washington

Abstract Background Methods Maps & Results Findings References

Abstract

Placeholder. This project investigates partisan gerrymandering at the state legislative level using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) simulation to generate thousands of legally valid alternative district maps. By comparing an enacted map's partisan distribution against this ensemble, we can quantify whether a map is a statistical outlier. The project also examines why geometric compactness metrics (e.g. Polsby–Popper scores) are unreliable indicators on their own.
